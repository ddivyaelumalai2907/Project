GOA

The state of Goa, in India, is famous for its beaches and places of worship.
Tourism is its primary industry,
and is generally focused on the coastal areas of Goa, with decreased tourist activity inland.
Major tourist attractions include Bom Jesus Basilica, Fort Aguada, a wax museum on Indian culture, and a heritage museum.
The Churches and Convents of Goa have been declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO.
Foreign language, mostly from Europe, arrive in Goa in winter, whilst the summer
and monsoon seasons see many Indian tourists.
Goa handled 2.29% of all foreign tourist arrivals in the country
